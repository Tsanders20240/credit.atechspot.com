Consumer rights

You can dispute inaccurate credit information yourself for free.

Credit-repair organizations do not have a special legal right to remove accurate information.

Core rights

  • You may contact consumer reporting agencies directly to dispute information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Consumer reporting agencies generally must investigate qualifying disputes.
  • Accurate, current, and verifiable information cannot lawfully be removed merely because it is unfavorable.
  • Covered credit-repair services require disclosures and a written contract.
  • Federal law provides cancellation rights for covered credit-repair contracts.
  • Credit-repair organizations may not make false or misleading statements.

Complaints and official help

CFPB complaint portal • FTC ReportFraud • IdentityTheft.gov

Before paying

Read all disclosures, the written agreement, exact services, payment timing, cancellation form, and privacy practices. Keep copies. Never sign blank documents.
