How to Review Your Credit Reports

Learn what appears in a credit report, how to compare bureau files, and how to document possible errors.

Start with official reports

Request reports through AnnualCreditReport.com. Review each bureau separately because the information may differ.

Check personal information

Review names, addresses, employers, and identifiers for information that does not belong to you.

Review every account

Compare ownership, status, balance, credit limit, payment history, dates, and remarks against your records.

Document possible errors

Save statements, letters, screenshots, payment confirmations, and identity-theft reports that support your position.

Dispute factually

State what is wrong, why it is wrong, and what correction you are requesting. Avoid blanket or false disputes.

Track responses

Keep copies, dates, confirmation numbers, and results from bureaus and furnishers.
